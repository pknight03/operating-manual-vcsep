Getting information for a referral

At times you may need to follow up with an organisation. For example if:

  • information is missing that you need as a liaison lead to arrange support at a local level
  • the information manager tells you more information is needed to arrange support at a regional level
  • a broker at regional level has asked for more information, to make a decision about whether they can help

3 questions that might be useful:

  • What information is being asked for and why? Write down any questions or specific details required.
  • When do they need the information by? Write down the dates.
  • How is it helping make a decision? Finding out how the information will help information managers and brokers reach decisions will help you understand what they are asking for.

By email

Use our email templates to request further information.

By phone

Use this checklist to get more context and detail on the request.

  1. Introduce yourself, saying you are calling from the Voluntary and Community Sector Emergency Partnership Get help as a local organisation service.
  2. Say is about the request [organisation name] submitted on [date].
  3. Confirm that the person you are talking to is the contact name on the form.
  4. Confirm the request for support.
  5. Explain that to progress the request you need further information on X Y Z.
  6. Ask any further questions and take notes.
  7. Give an indication of the length of time it will take for action to be taken and to provide the support, if you can.
  8. Thank the organisation.
  9. Ask if they would like an email to confirm the conversation outcome.
