Getting more information on a request

At times you may need to follow up with an organisation. For example if:

  • as a liaison lead you need more information to arrange support locally
  • the information manager tells you more information is needed to arrange support at a regional level
  • a broker needs more information to decide whether they can help

Questions to ask

3 questions that might be useful:

  1. What information is being asked for and why?
    Write down any questions or specific details required.
  2. When do they need the information by?
    Write down the dates.
  3. How is it helping make a decision?
    Finding out how the information will help information managers and brokers reach decisions will help you understand what they are asking for.

By email

Use our email templates to request further information.

By phone

Use this checklist to get more context and detail on the request.

  1. Introduce yourself, saying you are calling from the Voluntary and Community Sector Emergency Partnership 'Get help as a local organisation service.
  2. Say is about the request [organisation name] submitted on [date].
  3. Confirm that the person you are talking to is the contact name on the form.
  4. Confirm the request for support.
  5. Explain that to progress the request you need further information on [what you need information on].
  6. Ask any further questions and take notes.
  7. Give an indication of the length of time it will take for action to be taken and the support to be provided, if you can.
  8. Thank the organisation.
  9. Ask if they would like an email to confirm what you discussed in your conversation with them.
