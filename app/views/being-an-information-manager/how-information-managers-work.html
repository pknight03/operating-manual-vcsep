How information managers work

An information manager’s role involves:

  • Producing daily reports to be sent on to liaison leads for triage purposes.
  • Being the main point of contact for the liaison leads in their region regarding the status of different requests for support.
  • Communicating with brokers in order to grant access to the data they require in order to be able to action requests they have agreed to support with.
  • Identifying and reporting local insights and intelligence gathered from the ‘Get support as a local organisation’ service form to a regional and a national level for advocacy.
  • Supporting the regional tactical cell with information management, data collection, analysis, and visualisation.
  • Ensuring that information management sources and products are accurate, comprehensible, clear and simple.
  • Identifying the potential risks, harm and opportunities related to the collection of data for the VCS EP ‘Get support as a local organisation’ service.
  • Act as a controller to the data collected from the VCSEP ‘Get support as a local organisation’ service at a regional level, providing and removing access to the data where necessary.
  • Representing the Liaison Leads and hyper-local organisations information at the regional tactical cell meetings.
  • Ensuring that lessons learnt are captured and fed to the VCS EP members for future preparedness and response activities.
